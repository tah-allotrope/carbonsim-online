Executive Summary

Sprint 3 added the signature Kairosoft "life." It introduced a sprite-sheet frame-animation core the renderer never had, authored an original chibi citizen sheet, and spawned a pool of citizens that wander, animate, depth-sort with buildings, and react to game state (a compliant, clean city is busy; a shortfall or heavy smog empties the streets). Frontend-only; the 116-test suite still passes.

Two items were deferred to Sprint 4: a formal mobile FPS trace and the new-palette accessibility contrast pass. Recommendation: proceed to Sprint 4 (floating feedback juice + perf/a11y), the final sprint of the Kairosoft roadmap.

Background & Objective

Research flagged animated citizens as the most glaring missing Kairosoft element — and noted the renderer could only blit static frames. Sprint 3's objective: build the animation capability and bring the city to life with state-aware ambient citizens.

Inputs & Scope

  • Touched: scripts/gen_building_sprites.py (citizen sheet), web/assets/sprites/citizens.png, web/assets/manifest.json, web/js/isocity.js.
  • Out of scope: floating feedback (Sprint 4), engine/snapshot changes, projection change.

Assumptions & Constraints

  • Citizen sheet: 16×16 frames, 4-frame walk × 3 variants; loaded via the existing manifest/AssetLoader.
  • Preserve the 30fps throttle, particle caps, and reduced-motion static fallback.
  • Citizens are ambient/decorative (the canvas is aria-hidden); state is still in text/badges.

Methodology

Same cadence: implement → verify live (frame-slice + meta, animation pixel-diff, formula replication) → phase report → commit & push.

Phase-by-Phase Analysis

Phase 01 — Animation core

drawSprite() (sub-rect blit + flip) and walkFrame() (wall-clock frame clock); original citizens.png (64×48) with frame metadata in the manifest.

Phase 02 — Citizens & movement

A pool that wanders within the city bounds, flips by direction, and is depth-sorted with buildings by screen-y in a single painter pass; spawned only when the desired count changes (no refresh teleport).

Phase 03 — Reactive & perf/a11y

Count scales with compliance + smog via incremental add/remove; capped at 14; reduced motion freezes movement and idles the frame.

Findings & Recommendation

  • Renderer can now animate sprite sheets — reusable for Sprint-4 effects.
  • Citizens walk, layer, and react — 8 when compliant/clean (4 plots), 5 under shortfall or heavy smog; scene animates (~2,900 px/600ms).
  • Stable + bounded — no teleport on refresh; ≤14 citizens; throttle intact.
  • Recommendation: proceed to Sprint 4 — floating feedback juice + perf/a11y (closes the roadmap; folds in the deferred contrast pass).

Implementation Path

  1. Sprint 4: floating coins/numbers + reaction emotes (reuse the .bubble style + the new frame/particle infra), wired to decision/year events.
  2. Then: perf hardening (mobile FPS trace) + the new-palette a11y contrast pass; roll the full stack across coop.html / summary.html.
  3. Engine follow-up (small, optional): expose per-company abatement/emissions so bot buildings + citizen reactivity reflect the whole city, not just the player.
