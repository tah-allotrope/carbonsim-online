Section 2

Logic Flow

The year loop now carries a feedback cycle. At year close, compliance outcomes move policy_stability. At the next year start, stability sets the cap modifier and may fire a crackdown or relief, multi-turn effects re-apply and decrement, and queued follow-on cards are force-drawn. Card resolution can set conditions, queue follow-ons, and register durable effects — closing the loop.

Section 3

Math / Algorithm Used

Policy stability update (year close)

delta  = (60 - stability) * 0.10           # mean reversion toward neutral
delta += 4   if compliance_rate > 0.80
delta += 2   if compliance_rate > 0.85
delta -= 6   if penalized_fraction > 0.30
delta -= 10  if an election_pressure shock fired this year
stability = clamp(stability + delta, 0, 100)

Mean reversion is the key stabiliser: without it, a structurally over- or under-compliant field drives stability to an absorbing 0 or 100. Reverting 10% toward 60 each year gives the outcome deltas something to push against, producing a meter that drifts rather than latches.

Cap modulation (year start)

cap_modifier = clamp(1 + ((stability - 70) / 100) * 0.15, 0.88, 1.10)
current_cap  = total_baseline * allocation_factor * cap_modifier

The modifier is exactly 1.0 at the starting stability of 70, so a fresh game reproduces the Sprint 1–3 balance to the cent; it only diverges as stability drifts. The 0.15 coefficient and tight clamp were chosen after the first (0.30, wide-clamp) attempt produced a death-spiral (see Errors).

State-weighted draw

weight = base_weight
       * 2.0  if category == crisis      and stability < 40
       * 2.0  if category == opportunity and stability > 75
       * 1.5  if category == policy       and stability < 30

Cascading card resolution

  • sets_condition → append flag to active_conditions.
  • requires_condition → card ineligible until the flag is present (gate in _prereqs_met).
  • follow_on_cards → push ids to pending_card_injections; next draw_cards force-draws them via CardDeck.take_by_id.
  • effect_duration = D → apply once now, queue active_effects with remaining_years = D − 1.

Section 6

Limitations / Second-Best Alternative

  • Cap modulation, not replacement. The plan suggested fully replacing the scripted allocation factors with a stability formula. Instead, stability modulates the existing factor schedule (modifier neutral at 70). This was deliberate: it preserves the carefully-tuned per-scenario decline curves and the Sprint 1–3 balance, and keeps every existing cap test valid. The pure-formula version is the rejected second-best — cleaner on paper, but it would have discarded calibrated content and broken regression tests.
  • Auto-triggers are engine-side, not deck-drawn. Crackdown/relief fire deterministically from the engine and also have matching narrative cards gated by the condition. This avoids a hard dependency on deck plumbing in _start_year, at the cost of a small double-signal (engine effect + card) when both land.
  • Stability still trends low in the all-bot sweep. Because several bot strategies intentionally under-comply, the sweep field keeps penalised-fraction above 30%, so stability settles low (~2–15). This is correct for a stress test of mixed strategies; a human game with a compliant player sees a far gentler meter.
  • Follow-on injection is best-effort. If an injected card id is no longer in the draw pile (already drawn this run), it is silently dropped rather than reshuffled back in.
Section 7

Errors / Warnings / Flags Encountered

Bug — policy-stability death-spiral broke Sprint 3 balance
First implementation regressed the Sprint 3 dominance test: aggressive won 90% and net worths went deeply negative. Two compounding ratchets were at fault — (1) low stability tightened the cap, causing more shortfall → lower stability → tighter cap (positive feedback), and (2) the auto-crackdown used cbam_threat, which permanently multiplies penalty_rate; the eligible policy_crackdown card was then re-drawn every year, ratcheting the rate from 1000 → 1571. Fixed by: softer cap coefficient (0.30 → 0.15) with a tight clamp, mean-reverting stability, and switching both the trigger and the card to a non-ratcheting allowance_withdrawal. Re-verified: sweep max win rate back to ~40%, no dominant strategy.
Flag — missing preview launcher
.claude/launch.json referenced scripts/preview_run.py, which did not exist. Created it (with project-root sys.path insertion + chdir so server imports resolve regardless of the launcher's cwd).
Flag — badge CSS class name
The Policy Climate "warning" state initially used a non-existent badge-amber class; corrected to the existing badge-orange.
Section 8

Open Questions / Next Phase Seeds

  • Agent reaction to the climate. Sprint 3 agents tolerate the dynamic cap but don't yet strategise around crackdowns. Should CompanyAgent.plan_year pre-empt a looming crackdown (stability nearing 30) by over-buying ahead of the tightening?
  • Author the remaining chains. The plan targeted ~20 follow-on cards across categories; 10 are authored. The mechanic is fully wired, so the rest is content authoring.
  • Surface conditions in the UI. active_conditions are in the snapshot and the badge tooltip, but a dedicated "active conditions / effects" panel would make multi-turn state legible to the player.
  • Sprint 5 (Meta) interaction. Capex abatement + loans + XP unlock tree will lean on policy_stability (e.g. tech-failure cards, relief-driven unlock pacing) — the cascading-card schema is ready to carry those.
  • Per-difficulty stability tuning. The meter is calibrated on solo_standard; solo_easy / solo_hard may want different mean-reversion targets or trigger thresholds.
