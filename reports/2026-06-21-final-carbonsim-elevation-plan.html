Executive Summary

CarbonSim Online started this elevation as a functional but shallow compliance sim: static offset prices, three simplistic bot profiles, and no consequence preview before decisions. The five-sprint elevation plan targets Kairosoft-level mechanical depth — a 20–40 min session with compulsion-generating replayability, authentic carbon market instruments, and visible decision consequences.

What is done: Sprint 1 wired reproducible seeded state, a pure project_outcome consequence-preview function, and a year-end narrative modal. Sprint 2 replaced the static offset price with a BazaarBot-style demand-responsive model (ε=0.4, supply cap 50 t) and surfaced forward contracts and VCM project investment in the solo UI. Playtest validation across 8 seeds produced a coefficient of variation of 5.1% in penalty outcomes — well inside the <30% balance threshold, confirming the elasticity model is well-calibrated.

What remains: Sprints 3–5 deliver goal-driven AI agents with multi-year planning horizons, state-reactive cascading event cards driven by a policy_stability field, and persistent meta-progression via an XP unlock tree with capex abatement and jurisdiction skins.

Recommendation
Proceed to Sprint 3 (AI agents) immediately. The engine architecture (pure reducer + audit_log + snapshot) has proven stable across both sprints and absorbs new mechanics cleanly. Sprint 3 is the highest-leverage next investment: without multi-year AI planning, the new market instruments can still be dominated by simple heuristic play.
Section 3

Background & Objective

CarbonSim Online is a FastAPI + vanilla-JS carbon compliance management game. The core loop: manage emissions, acquire allowances via ETS auctions, buy offsets, and survive multi-year cap decline as a Vietnamese industrial company competing against AI bots.

The elevation request: "Elevate CarbonSim Online to Kairosoft/Sid Meier/tycoon sophistication level while deeply reflecting carbon market domain mechanics."

The architecture was already sound — an OpenRCT2-style deterministic reducer (apply_company_decision), immutable audit log, and snapshot system. The plan's research confirmed this is the correct architecture at 8-company scale and should not be changed. Every sprint therefore adds mechanics on top of the reducer rather than replacing it.

Session date range covered by this report: 2026-06-21 (single-day sprint pair).

Section 6

Methodology

The elevation plan follows a leverage-per-effort sequencing strategy derived from the research brief: determinism and legibility first, market mechanics second, AI agents third, story texture fourth, meta-progression last. Each phase must pass a seeded playtest batch before the next phase begins.

Design Framework

  • Reducer-first architecture — All state transitions go through apply_company_decision(state, action, payload) → state. No direct mutation. This makes every sprint's additions auditable and reversible.
  • Pure consequence preview — project_outcome(state, company_id, action, payload) deep-copies state, applies the action arithmetically, and discards the copy. The UI calls this on hover to show compliance/cash projections before commitment. No network round-trip.
  • BazaarBot demand-feedback model — Offset price responds to within-year aggregate demand: P(d) = P₀ × (1 + ε × min(d/S, 1)). Resets each year so price pressure is annual, not cumulative. The linear model was chosen over exponential to keep client-side preview arithmetic identical to the engine.
  • Monte-Carlo balance validation — 8-seed playtest batch after each sprint. Target: coefficient of variation of penalties <30% across seeds (confirming no single dominant strategy).
  • Phase-state machine — LOBBY → YEAR_START → DECISION_WINDOW → COMPLIANCE → COMPLETE. All new mechanics hook into existing phase transitions; no phase machine changes required through Sprint 2.

Complete Commit c6a9a6f

What was delivered: BazaarBot demand-responsive offset price with offset_demand_this_year accumulator; resets each _start_year. Forward contracts (buy_forward action) with 5% term premium, resolving in _start_year(delivery_year). Three VCM projects via invest_vcm action: REDD+ Forest (8 t/yr, $150k), Rice Paddy Methane (5 t/yr, $80k), Clean Cookstoves (3 t/yr, $50k). Balance result: 8-seed playtest batch produced penalty CV = 5.1% (target <30%). No single instrument dominated. The elasticity model at ε=0.4 with S=50t supply cap produces meaningful price pressure without runaway pricing.

OTC bilateral trades: TASK-02-06 (OTC solo UI) was noted in the plan as optional for Sprint 2 and was deferred — the engine already supports propose_trade/respond_to_trade but the solo UI panel was not prioritized over forward/VCM delivery. This is a clean Sprint 3 pick-up as AI agents will need to propose trades.

Tradeoffs Made

  • 5% vs 15% forward premium — Chose 5% to keep forwards usable under the annual price reset model. At 15%, the premium exceeded typical year-over-year spot variation, making forwards strictly dominated. Risk: if spot rarely rises >5%, forwards are trivially optimal. Sprint 3 strategy sweep will validate.
  • Supply cap as constant (50t) vs per-scenario — Used a single default to simplify initial calibration. Larger solo scenarios may need a higher cap. Exposed in SCENARIO_PACKS for per-scenario tuning.
  • OTC solo UI deferred to Sprint 3 — The engine supports propose_trade/respond_to_trade but the solo panel was deprioritized. Forward/VCM were higher-value user-facing additions. The AI agent sprint (Sprint 3) naturally drives OTC surface work anyway.
  • ALT: BazaarBot full agent market rejected — Multi-agent ask/bid clearing adds significant complexity for 8 companies. The demand-accumulator model achieves the same negative feedback loop in ~20 lines.
  • ALT: LLM year-end narrative rejected — Introduces API dependency and non-determinism. Template-driven text covers 80% of the case and is deterministic, offline, and maintenance-free.

Recommendation

Proceed to Sprint 3 — AI Agents
The market mechanics are calibrated and balanced. Sprint 3 is the highest-impact next step because: (1) without multi-year AI planning, players can still trivially outperform bots by using the new instruments the bots ignore; (2) AI-initiated OTC trade proposals naturally drive the OTC solo panel that was deferred; (3) the strategy sweep in Sprint 3 validates that Sprint 2's calibration holds under intelligent opposition. Do not skip to Sprint 4 — story cards built on top of naive bots will feel hollow.
