Math / Algorithm Used
Dynamic VCM Spot Price (BazaarBot-style)
P(d) = P₀ × (1 + ε × min(d / S, 1))

P₀ = base_price (scenario default)
ε = offset_price_elasticity = 0.4
d = offset_demand_this_year (accumulated)
S = annual_offset_supply_cap = 50 t

Examples (P₀ = $28):
d=0 → $28.00
d=20 → $28 × 1.16 = $32.48
d=40 → $28 × 1.32 = $36.96
d=50 → $28 × 1.40 = $39.20 (ceiling)

The min(d/S, 1) clamp prevents runaway prices above full-supply-consumed levels. The accumulator resets to 0 each _start_year call, so price pressure is annual not cumulative.

Forward Contract Pricing
spot_price = P(demand_at_time_of_purchase)
locked_price = round(spot_price × 1.05, 2)
total_cost = quantity × locked_price
delivery_year = current_year + 1

The 5% term premium reflects counterparty and duration risk. Forward contracts pay at purchase; credits arrive at _start_year(delivery_year) and are immediately usable for that year's compliance.

VCM Project Credit Stream
total_credits = annual_credits × duration_years

At each _start_year(y):
 if project.remaining_years > 0:
  offset_holdings += annual_credits
  remaining_years -= 1

First credit: year = invested_year + 1
Cost: paid once at invest_vcm call

Credits are not forward contracts — they arrive unconditionally each year until the project matures. They are added to offset_holdings before compliance is checked.

Limitations / Second-Best Alternatives
Limitation — Forward delivers offsets, not allowances

The forward contract delivers VCM credits (adds to offset_holdings) rather than ETS allowances. Real-world carbon forwards can be EUA forwards (allowance delivery) or VCM forwards. Implementing EUA forwards would require auction mechanics changes and was deferred to Sprint 3.

Limitation — No secondary market for contracts

Forward contracts cannot be sold back or transferred between companies in the current implementation. In real VCM markets, forwards are tradeable. Sprint 3 multi-agent work could surface this via OTC propose_trade extensions.

Alternative — Server-side consequence preview for forwards

The original plan specified a server-side GET /project call for all previews. The implemented approach extends client-side JS instead. This is strictly faster (zero latency) and avoids race conditions when live price changes between preview and commit.

Alternative — Per-scenario VCM catalogs

A single VCM_CATALOG constant is shared across all scenarios. The plan considered scenario-specific catalogs (e.g. higher-credit REDD+ under generous scenario). Deferred: the shared catalog is correct for now, and the lookup already uses pack.get("vcm_catalog", VCM_CATALOG) so overriding is trivial in Sprint 5.

Q3 — Supply cap calibration for large solo scenarios

annual_offset_supply_cap=50t is calibrated for 3-company multiplayer. Solo-hard has 5 companies with up to 5 bot agents all buying offsets, which could saturate the 50t cap quickly and spike prices. Consider raising annual_offset_supply_cap for 5-company scenarios.

Currently a company can invest in all three VCM projects simultaneously. Total annual credits = 8+5+3 = 16 t/yr, which can significantly overshoot the offset usage cap. Consider a per-company VCM credit cap or offset cap interaction check at invest time.
